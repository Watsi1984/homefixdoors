What is a composite door?

Composite doors are constucted using a combination or 'composite' of materials that make them far superior to standard doors.

They are designed to eliminate problems common to wooden doors, including weathering, colour fade, poor energy efficiency, fragility, and period maintenance and replacement.

Composite doors feature a steel security mesh, a hard-wearing PVC outer frame for optimum levels of thermal performance and strength, a reinforced structure, and hinges and locks designed to thwart burglars.

Composite doors are A-rated for thermal efficiency and will help to retain the heat in the home thanks to an insulating core. They are also precision-manufactured so the door fits perfectly in the frame.

There is a huge variety of door styles, colours, and glass design for maximum kerb appeal and the rugged design makes tiresome warping, cracks, and painting and thing of the past - composite doors can last decades with little or no maintenance.
